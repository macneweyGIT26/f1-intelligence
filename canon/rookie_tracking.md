## Tracking Protocol (Universal)

### Weekly Data Collection

**Per race, record:**

| Metric | Driver | Comparison Point | Signal Value |
|--------|--------|---|---|
| **Qualifying position vs teammate** | P? | Teammate P? | Speed differential (one-lap pace learning) |
| **FP1/FP2 improvement trend** | Lap time R1 → R2 → R3 | Teammates' improvement rate | Learning velocity (is driver adapting practice to race?) |

| **Mechanical DNF vs driver error** | DNF type | Similar cars' reliability | Is learning causing crashes or car reliability issue? |

| **Gap to teammate over season** | R1 gap: +0.5s | R3 gap: +0.3s | Is gap shrinking (learning working) or widening (struggling)? |

## Track-Specific Learning Expectations

### High-Technical Tracks (Suzuka, Monaco, Singapore)
**Rookie/developmental expectation:** Struggle. Setup-sensitive. Few practice laps available (sprint weekends even worse).

**What to monitor:**
- Gap to teammate widens? (Expected on technical tracks for less-experienced driver)
- Mechanical errors increase? (Overdriving from pressure)
- Does gap shrink race-day vs qualifying? (Learning happening in real-time)

**Betting adjustment:**
- Don't punish rookie for bad result on Suzuka. Tall order for learning driver.
- Points finish (top 6) is good target, not podium.
- Example: Bearman at Suzuka = expect underperformance. Don't overweight.

### Low-Technical / High-Power Tracks (Monza, Mexico, Spa)
**Rookie/developmental expectation:** Better relative performance. Power matters more than setup precision.

**What to monitor:**
- Does rookie/developmental driver close gap to teammate on power circuits?
- If yes: Skill is not pace (learning well) but confidence on technical circuits.
- If no: Systemic pace gap (harder to close through learning).

**Betting adjustment:**
- High-power circuits are less punishing. Bet them with more confidence if they're struggling on technical tracks.

### Street Circuits (Miami, Monaco, Singapore)
**Rookie/developmental expectation:** Precision test. One mistake = DNF or position loss.

**What to monitor:**
- DNF rate vs teammate (learning causing crashes?)
- Qualifying position vs race result (can they hold position or do they lose it?)
- Does home-track advantage help? (e.g., Bearman at Silverstone vs non-home street circuits)

**Betting adjustment:**
- Street circuits reveal driver maturity. If rookie crashes multiple times, that's a signal.
- If rookie holds position and executes clean, confidence builds for future rounds.
