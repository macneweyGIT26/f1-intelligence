## Overview

**Three independent betting markets with different risk/reward profiles:**

1. **Driver Markets** — Individual driver performance (winner, podium, points finish, top 6)
2. **Driver Pair Markets** — Both drivers from same team score/finish in position range
3. **Constructor Markets** — Team aggregate performance (team winner, both drivers score)

Each market has **different information asymmetries, different cash-out windows, different edge opportunities.**

---

## Market 1: Individual Driver Markets

### Market Characteristics
- **Coverage:** Winner, podium, top 6, points finish (top 10)
- **Odds volatility:** Highest. Reprices frequently (every practice session, qualifying, first lap)
- **Cash-out availability:** Constant. Best cash-out window post-qualifying, post-first-lap
- **Information decay:** Fast. Odds compress as new data emerges (FP1/FP2, qualifying, race conditions)

### Edge Opportunities
**Early-week stale prices (Mon/Tue):**
- Full market suite available (podium, top 6, points finish, straight forecasts)
- Market unknowns high (mechanical reliability, weather, strategy)
- Odds reflect pre-practice expectations (wide, inefficient)
- **Attack window:** Deploy heavy. Full budget mix. All three cash-out tiers active.

**Mid-week conditional (Wed/Fri):**
- Mechanical unknowns resolve (if car runs full session, reliability suspected)
- Exotic props (points finish, forecasts) disappear by Friday
- Top-finish markets persist (winner, podium, group betting)
- **Attack window:** FP2 long-runs show reliability. If positive, add to points-finish plays.

**Race-week (Sat/Sun):**
- One market group remains (Group Betting 1 = top finishers)
- First-lap odds release Sun morning (final betting window)
- Line compression complete. Odds nearly efficient.
- **Attack window:** First-lap leader only (final edge opportunity). Lock in cash-outs.

### Cash-Out Rules (Individual Driver)
- **Top tier (Kimi/Russell/Max):** Cash out 1x at 2x stake offer
- **Mid tier (Leclerc/Piastri/Hamilton):** Cash out 1x at 3x stake offer
- **Tail plays (Bearman/Bortoleto/Lindblad):** Cash out 1x at 2x stake offer, ride 2nd ticket

### Season-Long Betting Pattern
- **R1–R3:** Discover who's real vs overhyped. Tail plays at pocket stakes. Veterans baseline plays.
- **R4–R6:** Trends emerge. Rookie learning curves clear. Escalate stakes on confident thesis (2–3x R1–R3 allocation).
- **R7+:** Model locked. Attack undervalued odds with full confidence. Veterans' odds lagging performance.

## Market 2: Driver Pair Markets (Both-Drivers-to-Score)

### Market Characteristics
- **Coverage:** Both drivers from same team finish in points (top 10), top 6, or podium range
- **Odds presentation:** Both-drivers-to-score, Driver 1 + Driver 2 to podium, etc.
- **Odds volatility:** Medium. Reprices less frequently than individual (needs both drivers to cooperate)
- **Cash-out availability:** Lower. Only available certain weeks/tracks (vanishes like exotic props)

### Edge Opportunities
**Mechanical reliability insight:**
- Both-to-score implies **both cars must finish + both be fast enough.**
- If one car has reliability issues → both-to-score odds widen unfairly (market overpenalizes)
- Example: McLaren R2–R3 had gearbox issues. Both-Norris-Piastri-to-score offered too high odds (one car dying = bet void)

**Team strategy coordination:**
- Some teams sacrifice one driver for other's result (Ferrari strategy: Leclerc gets priority)
- Both-to-score assumes equal strategy priority (often untrue)
- If you know team's strategy bias → exploit odds

**Track-specific both-driver probability:**
- Technical tracks (Suzuka, Monaco) = harder for both to finish top 10 (fewer finishers)
- Flowing tracks (Australia, Japan) = easier (more mid-field finishes)
- Street circuits (Miami) = both-driver coordination harder (pit-stop timing, safety car bunching)

### Sportsbook Availability Pattern
- **Mon/Tue:** Full suite. Both-to-score for every team offered at various point-finish thresholds
- **Wed/Thu/Fri:** Subset. Only top constructor teams (Mercedes, Ferrari, McLaren) still offered. Mid-field pairs disappear
- **Sat:** One or two pairs remain. Best odds already vanished.
- **Post-race:** New market opportunity if team shows surprise performance (cash-out value)

### Cash-Out Rules (Driver Pairs)
- **Both-to-score (top 10):** 3x stake offer. Medium confidence. Points finish both likelyish.
- **Both-to-score (top 6):** 4x stake offer. Higher bar. Both top-6 rarer than both top-10.
- **Both-to-podium:** 5x+ stake offer. Extreme odds, very low probability. Only bet if team dominant (Mercedes R3).

### When to Bet Both-Drivers-to-Score

**Alpine (Ocon + Gasly):**
- Early week: Skip. Team dysfunction means strategy chaos. One driver often sacrificed.
- If both-to-score offered: High odds reflect real risk. Don't chase.

**Audi/Racing Bulls:**
- Early week: Better odds than Alpine (team improving, more stable).
- If both-to-score offered at +200 to +400: **Bet it.** Improving team, strategy likely coordinated, points finishes likely by R3+.

**Mercedes (Russell + Antonelli):**
- Early week: Dominant team. Both-to-score @ -200 or better = lock play. High confidence.
- R3 Japan: Mercury likely to 1-2 the race (both in top 6 guaranteed). If both-to-podium offered, evaluate vs individual plays (may be better EV).

**McLaren (Norris + Piastri):**
- R1–R2: Mechanical unknowns high. Both-to-score risky despite strong pace.
- R3+: If mechanical proved, both-to-score improves. Both-top-6 becomes core play.

### Season Progression (Both-Drivers-to-Score)

| Phase | Team Fitness | Both-to-Score Strategy | Odds Quality |
|---|---|---|---|
| **R1–R2** | Unknown | Avoid unless dominant (Mercedes). Risk: one car DNF. | Wide/inefficient (good early week value) |
| **R3–R4** | Mechanical clear | Core play if team proved reliable. | Tightening (compress mid-week) |
| **R5+** | Confident | Attack if team ascending (McLaren improving). | Near-efficient (less edge) |

## Market 3: Constructor Markets (Team Winner, Both-to-Score)

### Market Characteristics
- **Coverage:** Team (constructor) winner, both drivers to score, team podium
- **Odds volatility:** Low. Reprices slowly (team-level data emerges slower than driver data)
- **Cash-out availability:** Moderate. Available most weeks, but pricing less dynamic than driver markets
- **Information decay:** Slow. Constructor market reflects season-long trends, not round-by-round noise

### Edge Opportunities
**Structural advantage not yet priced:**
- Example: Mercedes overweight with FIA ballast, but best engineering. By R4, if they've shed ballast and fixed issues → constructor odds still reflect R1 disadvantage.
- **Attack:** If you know ballast sheds, bet Mercedes constructor at R5+ when odds lag reality.

**Team momentum vs individual driver variance:**
- Constructor market smooths individual driver luck (Hamilton lucky, Leclerc unlucky cancels out)
- Long-term trends emerge clearer: Is Ferrari ascending? Is McLaren fixing reliability?
- **Attack:** If trend is clear (McLaren improving) but odds lag, constructor plays beat individual driver bets.

**Underdog constructor chaos plays:**
- If Mercedes/Ferrari have mechanical DNF week, underdogs (Red Bull, McLaren) briefly favored
- Constructor odds may overcorrect (panic market)
- **Attack:** Post-DNF week, constructor odds of recovering team offer value (confidence returning week-by-week)

### Sportsbook Availability Pattern
- **Mon/Tue:** Team winner, both-to-score for each constructor
- **Wed+:** Same markets persist (less volatility, less need to prune)
- **Race day:** Still available. Line moves less (market confidence higher)

### Cash-Out Rules (Constructor)

| Scenario | Market | Cash-Out Trigger | Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Dominant (Mercedes R3)** | Constructor winner | 2x stake | Lock early. Ride second for full upside. |
| **Improving (McLaren R3+)** | Constructor winner | 3x stake | Medium confidence. Let trend mature. |
| **Underdog (Red Bull)** | Constructor winner | 4x+ stake | High odds. Tail play. Cash out if offer hits 2x. |
| **Any team** | Both-drivers-to-score | 3–4x stake per points threshold | Medium confidence. Track reliability track record. |

## Market Selection by Round

### Round 1–3: Full Experimentation
**Available markets:** All three (driver, pairs, constructor)
**Strategy:** Deploy across all three. Driver markets get heavy early-week budget. Pairs get selective plays (dominant teams or improving mid-field). Constructor gets baseline.
**Cash-outs:** Frequent (driver market reprices fastest)

### Round 4–6: Trend Clarification
**Available markets:** All three, but driver pair markets shrink (exotics disappear by Fri)
**Strategy:** Shift heavier to constructor as trends lock in. Driver pairs for proven teams (Mercedes, Ferrari) only. Driver plays for tail opportunities (rookie learning curves).
**Cash-outs:** Less frequent. Constructor plays mature slower.

### Round 7+: Model Locked
**Available markets:** All three (full suite back for remainder of season)
**Strategy:** Attack highest-confidence market. If driver learning curves proved, tail plays earn. If team trends clear, constructor plays earn. If mechanical proved, both-pair plays earn.
**Cash-outs:** Selective. Only lock in when confidence extremely high (3x+ offer).

## Real-World Example (R3 Japan)

**Monday early-week (best window):**
- Driver markets: Deploy $12 across Kimi, Leclerc, Hamilton, Hadjar tail plays
- Pair markets: $3 on "both Mercedes to score" (dominant), skip "both Ferrari to score" (ERS constraints)
- Constructor: $2 on Mercedes constructor winner (baseline)
- **Total: $17 of $40 budget deployed**

**Wednesday FP2 (conditional):**
- FP2 shows McLaren reliability solid (no gearbox issues) → Add $2 to "both McLaren top 6"
- Ferrari ERS constraint confirmed (same as R1) → Skip new Ferrari pair plays
- **Total: +$2 deployed**

**Saturday post-quali:**
- Kimi pole validated. Odds compress from +300 to -180.
- Cash out 1x Kimi ticket ($4.50 offer, covers $4 stake + profit)
- Russell +175 now available (secondary Mercedes). Add $2 as hedge if desired.
- Both Mercedes to podium offered at -180 (high confidence). Add $1 if it hits 3x by Sunday.
- **Total: +$0 to +$3 decision**

**Sunday race morning:**
- First-lap odds drop. Kimi -180 (heavy favorite), Russell +150 (secondary), Leclerc +1200 (value)
- Remaining budget: ~$14 (after $6 first-lap anchor decision)
- Deploy $2 on first-lap leader (Russell or Leclerc chaos play), hold $12 for live race decisions

**Result:** Mixed betting across three markets, cash-out windows timed correctly, budget recycled as each market matures.

## Carry-Forward Rules

1. **Early-week deploys across all three markets** (driver gets 60%, pairs 25%, constructor 15%)
2. **Mid-week adds conditionally** (FP2 triggers mechanical plays, pairs if reliability proved)
3. **Race-week locks cash-outs** (no new bets Sat/Sun except first-lap)
4. **Driver pair markets disappear by Fri** (don't plan on late-week access)
5. **Constructor markets persist** (can add mid-season as trends lock)
6. **Cash-out tiers differ by market:** Driver 2–3x, Pair 3–4x, Constructor 2–3x
7. **No market is "always" best** (edge rotates with information emerges: driver early, pair mid, constructor late)
