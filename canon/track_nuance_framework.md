## Overview

Every track has three attributes that determine betting strategy and driver-performance expectations. These are NOT subjective commentary. They are **decision filters** that immediately tell you:

- How much driver skill matters vs car pace
- Whether grid position locks the race
- How often chaos (safety car, weather, incidents) rewrites the outcome

## Track Classification System

### Technical Rating
**Definition:** How much does driver skill + setup finesse determine the result vs raw car pace?

| Rating | Characteristic | Examples | Betting Implication |
|--------|---|---|---|
| **Low** | Power matters more than precision. Smooth corners. Overtaking straightforward. | Monza, Mexico, Spa (first sector) | Experienced drivers + strong PU > young drivers. Haas underperforms. Russell excels. |
| **Medium** | Mixed. Some precision sections, some power sections. | Australia, Canada, Japan (flowing sections) | Balanced field. Both setup and driver matter. |
| **High** | High downforce required. Tight line discipline. Setup heavily weighted. | Suzuka (overall), Silverstone, Singapore (first 2/3) | Young drivers struggle. Home-track advantage high. Bearman expects underperformance. |
| **Extreme** | Millimeter margins. One mistake = catastrophe. Setup dominant. | Monaco, Singapore (Turn 1), street circuits | Qualifying nearly = race. Driver experience > raw pace. Precision > speed. Chaos probability high. |

### Qualifying Importance
**Definition:** Does grid position determine the race outcome, or can undercut/strategy/overtaking flip it?

| Rating | Characteristic | Examples | Betting Implication |
|--------|---|---|---|
| **Low** | Qualifying nearly irrelevant. Undercut/overcut wins races. Long DRS trains. | Spa (long to first corner), Monza (slipstream) | First-lap leader market weak. Pit strategy bets strong. Mid-field chaos plays high value. |
| **Medium** | Qualifying matters but not destiny. Strategy can flip top 3. | Australia, Japan (flowing) | Qualifying gives edge but not lock. Race strategy bets valuable. |
| **High** | Grid position heavily weighted. Strategy limited by track. Passing rare. | Suzuka, Silverstone, Canada | Qualifying positions sticky. First-lap leader important. Top-6 locks likely. |
| **Extreme** | Qualifying = race. Passing nearly impossible. | Monaco, street circuits, Singapore | Grid P1–P3 nearly locks podium. First-lap leader = race winner. Cash-out early. |

### Chaos Probability
**Definition:** How often does safety car, weather, accidents, or first-lap incidents scramble the expected result?

| Rating | Characteristic | Examples | Betting Implication |
|--------|---|---|---|
| **Low** | Predictable. Few incidents. Safety car rare. Weather stable. | Bahrain, Abu Dhabi, Mexico | Favorite holds. Upsets rare. Tail plays weak. |
| **Medium** | Occasional incidents. Weather possible. Safety car happens. | Australia, Japan, Canada | Mid-week cash-outs valuable. First-lap insurance plays. |
| **Moderate-Low (Front-loaded)** | Chaos concentrated in first lap + tunnel. After lap 1 = procession. Safety car rare. | Monaco | **First-lap market is primary edge.** Chaos IS lap 1. After lap 1, race settled. Mid-race chaos plays weak. Cash-out winners post-lap-1 (grid locked). |
| **High** | Multiple incident probability throughout race. Weather common. Safety car bundling. | Singapore (humidity, rain risk) | Chaos plays strong. Underdog odds improve during race. Cash-out early if leading. |

## How to Use Track Tags

**Race morning workflow (before placing bets):**

1. **Pull track tags** (Technical/Qualifying/Chaos)
2. **Map to betting matrix:**
   - High technical → driver experience weighs more → Bearman underweighted, Russell overweighted
   - High qualifying importance → first-lap leader bets > strategy bets
   - High chaos → cash-outs more valuable, tail plays better odds
3. **Adjust cash-out thresholds:**
   - High qualifying importance + low chaos → cash out earlier (2x at top tier, easier to execute)
   - High chaos → hold bets longer (race offers better value post-safety-car)
4. **Evaluate Lindblad:**
   - Technical track + Lindblad outperforming Hadjar = skill edge signal
   - Power circuit + Lindblad underperforming = PU or setup issue at Red Bull (affects Verstappen model)
