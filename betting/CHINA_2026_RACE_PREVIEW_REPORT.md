## TYRE STRATEGY (LOCKED — Mar 11, 2026)

**Pirelli Nominations (Confirmed):**

| Compound | Designation | Usage |
|----------|-------------|-------|
| C4 | SOFT | Qualifying + sprint high-risk |
| C3 | MEDIUM | Sprint/race base strategy |
| C2 | HARD | Defensive + long-stint durability |

**Strategic Implications:**

### Soft (C4) — Qualifying Dominator
- **FP window (Fri):** Teams will lock C4 for qualifying pace runs
- **Sprint usage:** Risky 1-stop (18 laps = moderate deg)
- **Pit window:** ~8–10 laps depending on gap + pace delta
- **Overcut/undercut:** Possible if pit delta > pace delta
- **Risk:** One-off pace; high deg; no race use (typically)

### Medium (C3) — Race Workhorse
- **Expected usage:** Sprint 1-stop or split (C3 → C3)
- **Race strategy:** Likely 1-stop (C3 → C3 if clean, or C3 → C2 late)
- **Flexibility:** Can extend stints if conditions tighten or SC deployed
- **Pit window:** 20–30 laps depending on pace + tyre temp
- **Undercut difficulty:** Marginal; 2–3 sec gain possible

### Hard (C2) — Defensive Backup
- **Expected usage:** Race contingency only
- **Durability:** Designed for long stints; poor pace early
- **Deployment:** Late-race (final 10–15 laps) if fresher legs needed
- **Undercut potential:** Weak; only used in desperation (engine penalty recovery, comeback, etc.)

**Comparison to Australia (Round 1):**
- Australia: C5 (softest) for soft slot
- China: C4 (one stage harder) — suggests higher track temps or durability concern
