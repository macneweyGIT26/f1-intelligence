## 2026 ERS/BATTERY MANAGEMENT — THE HIDDEN TECHNICAL STORY

### Five Battery Regimes (every lap, every driver)

All 2026 cars operate in five energy modes:

1. **Deploy** — MGU-K adds power (battery drains), used on straights for extra pace
2. **Harvest-Braking** — kinetic energy recovered under brakes
3. **Lift and Coast** — early throttle lift, gentle harvesting into corners
4. **Super-Clipping** — harvesting at full throttle (car decelerates faster than drag alone explains; sacrifices top speed to bank energy)
5. **Idle** — no net battery change

### The Mercedes Advantage (Suzuka 130R)

**Mercedes best at super-clipping** — subtle high-speed energy recovery that doesn't lose much top speed.

Evidence from R3:
- Antonelli's top speed: 320+ km/h through 130R
- Ferrari (Leclerc): ~305 km/h (15 km/h slower at same throttle)
- Honda (Aston Martin): ~260 km/h (60 km/h clipping deficit at 130R)

**Why it matters:** Mercedes can recover energy at high speed without visibly slowing down. This buys strategic flexibility: run longer on tires, respond to safety cars, execute undercuts. Ferrari/Honda cannot.

### Gear Selection as Energy Tool (2026 Innovation)

Teams deliberately select lower gears at high speed to enable smoother energy harvesting:
- **Lower gear** = faster engine spin = MGU-K can harvest without large torque resistance
- **Higher gear** = engine spins slower = MGU-K needs larger resistance, visibly slowing the car

**Leclerc confirmed this at Suzuka T12/T13:**
- 2025: 7th gear, continuous acceleration to 270 km/h
- 2026: 6th gear, acceleration fades to 250 km/h (intentional trade-off)
- **Purpose:** Preserve energy recovery capability while staying WOT, not a weakness

This explains Ferrari's top speed deficit (not aero, energy recovery philosophy).
