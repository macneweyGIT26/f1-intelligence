## Race Strategy Preview

**Expected Strategies:**

### Front Row (Antonelli, Russell)
- Likely: C4 (Medium) start → C3 (Hard) finish
- Alternative: C5 (Soft) start for track position → early pit lap 8-12 → C3 (Hard)
- Mercedes will want to control from the front — expect medium start

### Ferrari (Hamilton P3, Leclerc P4)
- Need an offset strategy to challenge Mercedes
- Possible: C5 (Soft) start → undercut attempt → C4 (Medium) → C3 (Hard)
- Risk: Sprint showed tyre wear issues — two-stop could backfire
- Better play: Mirror Mercedes on C4 → C3 and hope for safety car

### McLaren (Piastri P5, Norris P6)
- Standard C4 → C3 one-stop
- Gap to Ferrari is small enough that undercut might gain positions
- Norris may try opposite strategy to Piastri for team flexibility

### Verstappen (P8)
- Aggressive offset likely — needs to gain positions
- Possible: C5 (Soft) → early stop → C4 (Medium) → late C3 (Hard) two-stop
- Track position is everything at Shanghai — two-stop risky but P8 has nothing to lose

## ACTUAL RACE STRATEGY (Post-Race)

### Dominant Strategy: Medium → Hard (1-Stop)
Top 4 all ran identical M→H one-stop. No strategic variation at the front — Mercedes pace made it irrelevant.

| Driver | S1 Compound | Pit Lap | S2 Compound | Result |
|--------|-------------|---------|-------------|--------|
| Antonelli | Medium | ~Lap 15-17 | Hard | P1 (+5.5s) |
| Russell | Medium | ~Lap 15-17 | Hard | P2 |
| Hamilton | Medium | ~Lap 15-17 | Hard | P3 |
| Leclerc | Medium | ~Lap 15-17 | Hard | P4 |
| Bearman | Medium | ~Lap 15-17 | Hard | P5 (+5 places) |
| Gasly | Medium | ~Lap 15-17 | Hard | P6 |

### Alternative Strategies
- **Hadjar (S→H→H, 3-stop):** Went aggressive on softs early, but needed two hard stints. Mean pace 98.22s — significantly slower than one-stoppers.
- **Verstappen (S→H, 2-stop):** Started on softs from P8, attempted undercut. DNF lap 45 before strategy played out.
- **Colapinto (H→M):** Reverse strategy — started hard, finished on mediums. P10 result solid for Alpine's second car.
- **Lindblad (H→M→S):** Three-compound strategy. Aggressive but P12 finish suggests it didn't pay off.

### Safety Car Impact
Stroll's battery failure on lap 9 triggered Safety Car. This scrambled the field:
- Some pitted under SC (cheap stop), others stayed out
- Created a three-way fight for P2 behind Antonelli
- Russell eventually broke free; Hamilton vs Leclerc battled for P3

### Key Tyre Insight
- **Mercedes:** Lowest tyre degradation across both stints. Mean lap 96.46s (Antonelli) is absurd consistency.
- **Ferrari:** Higher deg on both compounds. Hamilton's last-lap 1:36.103 vs Antonelli's 1:36.929 — Ferrari faster at the end but only because Antonelli was managing.
- **The PU-deg connection:** Ferrari needs more power to run more downforce → less sliding → less tyre wear. Their chassis may actually be the best, but the PU deficit forces them to compensate aerodynamically, hurting tyres.
