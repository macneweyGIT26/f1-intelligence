# Australian GP 2026 — Aerodynamic & Top Speed Analysis

**Date:** Mar 8, 2026 (Australian GP)  
**Focus:** Active aero impact, drag reduction, DRS deployment, battery drain

---

## Key Findings: 2025 vs 2026 Drag

### 2025 Characteristics
- Very high drag baseline
- DRS deployment had **major impact** on top speed
- Top speed variance significant between DRS ON/OFF

### 2026 Characteristics
- **Minimal drag due to active aero** — Cars naturally slippery
- DRS still deployed but impact is **much reduced**
- Overtake-mode activation >290 km/h, but **battery drain limits top speed benefit**
- Battery depletion is the limiting factor, not aerodynamic drag

**Implication:** Battery management becomes critical for extended DRS use; teams must ration deployment strategically.

## Aero Philosophy for 2026

**Active Aero Deployment:**
- Minimizes base drag (advantage on all circuits)
- DRS effectiveness reduced (less delta when already slippery)
- Teams now optimize for:
  - Active aero angle/configuration
  - Power unit efficiency
  - Battery depletion rate under DRS

**Overtake Mode (>290 km/h):**
- Not a guaranteed top-speed gain (battery limits it)
- Effective for **sustained acceleration** rather than peak speed
- Requires **race-long battery planning**
