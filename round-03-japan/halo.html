Circuit — Suzuka International Racing Course

5.807 km | 53 laps | Figure-8 layout | Clockwise

Circuit Character

One of F1's greatest tracks. Figure-8 layout with flowing high-speed sections (Esses, 130R) and technical low-speed zones (hairpin, chicane). Rewards total car balance — aero, mechanical grip, and PU power all matter. High tyre degradation.

Key Corners

Esses (T3-T7): High-speed flowing section. Separates cars by aero quality.
Spoon (T13-T14): Double-apex, heavy tyre load. Rear stability critical.
130R (T15): ~300 km/h left-hander. Flat-out with 2026 cars.
Chicane (T16-T17): Only real overtaking zone with DRS into T1.

Mercedes Advantage

Suzuka rewards the best car across all speed ranges. Mercedes W17 has shown dominance in exactly these conditions. The Esses will be their playground. Expect 0.5s+ gap to Ferrari in qualifying.

Overtaking Difficulty

Active aero + DRS zones help, but Suzuka is still a qualifying-position track. Starting inside top 4 = near-guaranteed podium if reliable. Recovery drives from P8+ are harder here than China.
