## SUZUKA — THE HONDA HOME CIRCUIT

### Historical Context
Suzuka was Honda's proving ground for decades. Now Honda powers only Aston Martin while Red Bull (their former partner) makes their own engine. The politics are rich:
- **Honda (Aston Martin):** 3 DNFs from 4 starts. Their home race with zero points. Pressure is immense.
- **Red Bull Powertrains:** 2 PU failures in 2 races. At the circuit where Honda engineers still have influence and infrastructure. Maximum scrutiny.

### Circuit PU Demands
Suzuka has **two long straights** (main straight + back straight before 130R) and **sustained high-speed corners** that demand full ERS deployment. The 2026 PU split (ICE + ERS, near 50/50 power contribution) means:
- ERS harvesting through the Esses and Spoon is critical
- Battery management over a full lap is more demanding than Shanghai
- Clipping (running out of electrical energy) will be more visible here

### PU Performance Outlook

**Mercedes PU** — Dominant. Four teams powered but performance spread shows chassis is the differentiator. Suzuka's power demands should maintain their advantage. The ICE/ERS power-split debate may see Mercedes lobbying to maintain current regulations that favor their design.

**Ferrari PU** — Solid second-best. The gap at Suzuka may widen slightly (more power-dependent circuit) but harder tyre compounds could offset through better mechanical grip.

**Red Bull Powertrains** — Crisis. 2 PU failures in 2 races. Suzuka's demands on battery and ERS are higher than Shanghai. If the battery issue isn't resolved, expect more failures.

**Honda** — At their home circuit with 1 finish from 4 starts. Reputational damage is severe. They need both Aston Martins to finish regardless of position.

**Audi** — Steady. No PU failures but pace deficit to the field is significant. Suzuka's high-speed demands may expose them further.
