## CIRCUIT PROFILE — SUZUKA

**Key characteristics:**
- **Length:** 5.807 km — longest on calendar
- **Turns:** 18 — figure-8 layout, unique crossover bridge
- **Downforce:** HIGH — sustained high-speed corners (Esses, 130R, Spoon)
- **Tyre stress:** EXTREME — hardest compound selection (C1/C2/C3) confirms this
- **DRS/Active Aero zones:** 2 (main straight, back straight before 130R)
- **Overtaking difficulty:** VERY HIGH — limited braking zones, track position premium
- **Pit lane loss:** ~24-26 seconds (relatively high)

**Critical sections:**
- **Esses (T3-T7):** Flowing high-speed. Separates good aero from great. Mercedes W17's downforce advantage should shine here.
- **Degner curves (T8-T9):** High-speed direction change. Rear stability critical.
- **Spoon (T13-T14):** Long sustained corner. Tyre loading intense — will expose degradation differences.
- **130R (T15):** Near-flat-out. Requires absolute trust in aero. One of the fastest corners in F1.
- **Chicane (T16-T17):** Only real overtaking opportunity outside DRS zones.

**Why Suzuka matters for 2026:**
The figure-8 layout tests every aspect of the car — low-speed, high-speed, sustained loading, braking. It's the ultimate all-rounder test. Teams that are strong in one area but weak in another get exposed here.

## FORM GUIDE (Based on 2 Rounds)

### Tier 1 — Clear Favorites
**Mercedes** — 1-2 in both races. Mean pace gap of ~0.6s/lap to Ferrari. W17 has the best PU AND chassis. Suzuka's high-speed corners should amplify their aero advantage. Expect another front-row lockout.

### Tier 2 — Podium Contenders
**Ferrari** — Consistent 3-4 finishes. +0.64s/lap pace deficit. Suzuka's tyre demands (hardest compounds) could help them if their deg improves on harder rubber. Hamilton has Suzuka pedigree (6 wins). Key variable: can they close the PU gap on a power-hungry circuit?

### Tier 3 — Best of the Rest
**Alpine** — Gasly P7→P6 progression. Mercedes PU on a power circuit = advantage. Suzuka could suit them.
**Haas** — Bearman P10→P5 breakout in China. Ferrari PU. If McLaren's issues continue, Haas could consolidate P4 in WCC.
**Racing Bulls** — Lawson is a recovery drive specialist (+7 places China). RBPT reliability is the concern.

### Tier 4 — Unreliable / Too Slow
**McLaren** — Defending champions. Both DNS in China. Need to prove car works before they're bettable.
**Red Bull** — Verstappen DNF + P9 sprint. RBPT has 2 PU failures in 2 races. Avoid.
**Audi** — Hulkenberg competitive on pace but can't convert to points.

### DO NOT BET
**Aston Martin** — 3 DNFs from 4 starts. Honda PU reliability is zero. Both drivers pointless.
**Cadillac** — 3.5s/lap off pace. Finishes races but cannot score points.

## KEY QUESTIONS FOR SUZUKA

1. **Does the hardest tyre selection help Ferrari?** China's softer compounds exposed their degradation. C1/C2/C3 at Suzuka means less graining, more consistent. Could close the gap.
2. **Can McLaren start a race?** Both DNS in China is unprecedented for a defending champion. If they sort it, they have pace (Norris P6 quali in China).
3. **RBPT reliability at a Honda circuit?** Ironic — Red Bull makes their own engine now but Suzuka is Honda's home. Pressure to perform but 2 failures in 2 races is the trend.
4. **Overtaking at Suzuka?** Track position is king. Qualifying matters more here than any other circuit. First-lap positioning is critical.
5. **Weather?** Late March in Japan can bring rain. Suzuka in the wet is spectacular and chaotic.
